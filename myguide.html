GUIDE

What is weaning?

Weaning is the process of slowly switching a bay's diet from the mother's breast milk to regular food.


When should my baby start weaning?

It is suggested to start weaning at around four to six months. Weaning could take days, weeks or months.


What exactly is the weaning process?

What are the Stages of Weaning?

  • Stage 1: Introduction of solid foods - from around six months.
  • Stage 2: More textures and tastes - from around seven months.
  • Stage 3: Wider variety and family food - from around 9-12 months.

How often should I get checkups?

Weeks 4 to 28 1 prenatal visit a month
Weeks 28 to 36 1 prenatal visit every 2 weeks
Weeks 36 to 40 1 prenatal visit every week


As a parent, what factors should I be worried about?

  • Rh-incompatibility - Rh-negative mother and Rh-positive father
  • Blood group of previous pregnancy's baby - if Rh-positive for a Rh-negative mother
  • Age of parents
  • Thalassemia
  • Chromosomal Defects
  • Ante-Natal Screening
  • Other infectious diseases
  • Last Menstrual Period (LMP) and Expected Date of Delivery (EDD)


What factors should I be worried about for my baby?

  • Weight at time of birth
  • Weight gain
  • Growth Milestones
  • Blood Group
